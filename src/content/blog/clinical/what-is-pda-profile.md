Pathological Demand Avoidance (PDA) is a behavioural profile associated with the autism spectrum that is characterized by an intense, anxiety-driven need to avoid everyday demands and expectations.

## Why PDA Is Frequently Overlooked

Many individuals with a PDA profile appear highly verbal and socially motivated, which can mask autistic traits in standard screening tools. Traditional ADOS-2 and ADI-R instruments may not capture the demand-avoidance dimension well, leading to missed or delayed diagnoses.

At Twilight Psychology, our evaluators are trained to:

- Recognize the behavioral and cognitive signatures of PDA
- Supplement standardized tools with clinical interview data
- Provide a formal diagnostic opinion that acknowledges the PDA profile

## Our Assessment Process

1. **Intake interview** — We gather developmental history with a specific focus on demand avoidance patterns across home, school, and social settings.
2. **Cognitive and adaptive testing** — Standard measures supplemented with clinician observation.
3. **Collateral data** — Parent and caregiver questionnaires designed to capture PDA-specific behaviors.
4. **Feedback session** — We explain findings in plain language and discuss support strategies.

## Why This Matters

A formal PDA diagnosis opens access to appropriate educational accommodations, therapeutic strategies, and, where applicable, disability support services.
