Searching for **ADHD testing in Lexington, KY** usually means one of two things: a family is trying to understand a child's behavior at school or home, or an adult is trying to explain a pattern that has followed them for years without a clear name. Either way, the evaluation process starts with the same question — not "does this person have ADHD," but "what is actually going on, and what does this person need?"

At Twilight Psychology, ADHD evaluations are built around that second question. The goal is not to confirm or deny a label. It is to build a useful, accurate picture that supports real decisions about support, treatment, school planning, and daily life.

## What ADHD testing actually measures

An ADHD evaluation is not a single test. It is a clinical process that typically combines several components:

- **Clinical interview** — reviewing history, current concerns, symptoms across settings, and developmental context
- **Rating scales and behavioral questionnaires** — gathered from the person being evaluated, and often from parents, teachers, or partners depending on age
- **Cognitive and attention testing** — standardized measures of attention, working memory, processing speed, and executive function
- **Rule-out and clarification work** — distinguishing ADHD from anxiety, sleep problems, learning disabilities, autism, or other conditions that can present similarly
- **Feedback session** — a conversation where findings are explained in plain language along with specific next steps

The specific combination of measures depends on the referral question, the person's age, and what the clinician determines is most clinically relevant. A useful evaluation does not use the same battery for every person.

## ADHD evaluation for children versus adults

The referral questions are often different depending on age.

**For children**, families are usually seeking clarity about attention, impulsivity, hyperactivity, or regulation difficulties that are affecting school performance, peer relationships, or home life. A child evaluation also considers whether ADHD is the primary driver or whether anxiety, autism, learning differences, or trauma are better explanations — or whether multiple things are going on at once.

One important practical note: **ADHD is typically not diagnosable in children below age 5**. Preschool-age attention and activity levels vary significantly across normal development, and most evaluators wait until age 5 or 6 before applying formal ADHD criteria. If your child is younger and you have concerns, an evaluation can still be useful — it just focuses on developmental context, current functioning, and what support is appropriate now.

**For adults**, the referral question is often about chronic underperformance, difficulty sustaining focus, disorganization, emotional dysregulation, or impulsivity that has never had a formal explanation. Many adults arrive at evaluation after years of managing symptoms through workarounds, only to find that daily demands have finally outpaced those strategies. Late-identified ADHD in adults is common, especially in people whose intelligence or high-masking patterns helped them compensate into adulthood.

Adult ADHD evaluation at Twilight includes a developmental history component — because ADHD is a lifespan condition, and symptoms need to have been present before age 12 to meet diagnostic criteria. That does not mean you needed a diagnosis as a child. It means there should be a recognizable pattern going back to childhood, even if it was never noticed or named.

## A neuro-affirming approach to ADHD evaluation

ADHD evaluations at Twilight Psychology are conducted within a **neuro-affirming framework**. That matters for a few reasons.

First, it means the evaluation does not treat ADHD as a deficit to be eliminated. ADHD involves genuine differences in attention, activation, and executive function — and a good evaluation acknowledges both the challenges those differences create and the ways they can also be genuine strengths in the right context.

Second, it matters when ADHD and autism overlap — a presentation often called **AuDHD**. Traditional ADHD rating scales and structured testing do not always capture high-masking presentations accurately. Clinicians who understand neurodivergent masking can differentiate between what the scores show and what is actually going on clinically.

Third, it shapes the recommendations. An evaluation framed around "how does this person's brain work and what supports fit that brain?" produces more useful guidance than one framed as a yes-or-no checkbox. That includes thinking carefully about accommodations, therapy modalities, medication considerations, and environment fit.

## Insurance and self-pay options

Twilight Psychology accepts **most major insurance plans** for covered ADHD evaluations, including commercial plans, Kentucky Medicaid plans, and Medicare. Whether your specific plan covers ADHD testing — and how much — depends on your benefits and medical-necessity criteria.

Insurance-based evaluations involve a prior authorization or benefits review process that adds time before scheduling. Insurance also limits the number of testing units approved, which means clinicians focus the battery on the most clinically necessary measures.

For **self-pay evaluations**, scheduling is typically faster, with wait times around **3 weeks** for most clinicians. Reports are often completed within about 7 days following the evaluation.

## What happens after an ADHD evaluation

The evaluation ends with a **feedback session** where results are reviewed in plain language, and a written report is provided. That report typically includes the diagnostic conclusions, a summary of the testing findings, and specific recommendations.

For many people, the recommendations are the most valuable part. Depending on what the evaluation finds, recommendations may include:

- specific accommodations at school or work
- referrals for therapy, coaching, or medication management
- guidance for families on supporting an ADHD child at home
- clarity about whether ADHD is the primary concern or whether something else is also in play

If therapy is part of the next step, Twilight Psychology also offers individual therapy through clinicians experienced with ADHD. If medication management is part of the picture, [Nicola Allen, MSN](/about/clinicians/nicola-allen) provides psychiatric medication management services in Lexington.
