## About Medical Marijuana Consultations

Medical marijuana consultations are clinical evaluations to determine if a cannabis-based treatment plan is appropriate for your medical condition. Renewals are follow-up visits to assess ongoing treatment efficacy and adjust recommendations as needed.

## What Medical Marijuana Does

Medical marijuana contains cannabinoids (primarily THC and CBD) that interact with your body's endocannabinoid system. Depending on the strain and dosage, medical marijuana may:

- **Reduce chronic pain** by modulating pain signals
- **Decrease inflammation** associated with conditions like MS
- **Alleviate anxiety and hyperarousal** common in PTSD
- **Support symptom management** for cancer patients, including pain, nausea, and loss of appetite
- **Improve sleep quality** in many patients

The effects vary widely based on the cannabinoid profile (THC/CBD ratio), delivery method (edibles vs tinctures), dosage, and individual body chemistry. Our clinician will work with you to identify the optimal approach for your specific condition and symptoms.

## Medical Marijuana and ADHD Considerations

While not currently listed as a qualifying condition under Kentucky's medical marijuana program, **ADHD and cannabis use require careful consideration**. Some evidence suggests cannabis may temporarily reduce hyperactivity or impulsivity, but research also indicates potential risks:

- **Cognitive impact:** Regular use may affect working memory and focus—which are already challenged in ADHD
- **Dependency potential:** Individuals with ADHD may have higher risk of cannabis misuse or dependency
- **Interaction with medications:** Cannabis may interact with ADHD medications or other psychiatric treatments

If you have ADHD and are interested in medical marijuana for a co-occurring qualifying condition (e.g., PTSD, chronic pain), this will be discussed thoroughly during your consultation to ensure safety and appropriateness.

## Potential Consequences & Important Considerations

### Employment & Drug Testing

- **Federal employment:** Federal employers have strict cannabis policies; employees may be subject to termination regardless of state legalization
- **Private employment:** Many employers still conduct drug screenings; positive results may affect employment regardless of medical marijuana status in Kentucky
- **Safety-sensitive positions:** Positions involving heavy machinery, transportation, or security may have zero-tolerance policies
- **Testing detection:** THC can remain detectable in standard drug tests for days to weeks depending on use frequency and testing method
- **Smoking THC** Smoking medical marijuana is illegal in Kentucky

### Federal Legal Status

- **Federal law vs. state law:** Cannabis remains a Schedule I controlled substance at the federal level, despite Kentucky's medical marijuana program
- **Interstate travel:** Transporting medical marijuana across state lines is illegal federally
- **Military personnel:** Federal law prohibits military members from using cannabis, even if recommended at the state level

### Second Amendment & Gun Ownership

- **Federal prohibition:** Federal law prohibits any person who is a "user of a controlled substance" from possessing firearms or ammunition (18 U.S.C. § 922)
- **Medical marijuana users:** Even in legal states, individuals with active medical marijuana cards or who use cannabis may be prohibited from purchasing, possessing, or carrying firearms
- **Background checks:** Federal background check forms (ATF Form 4473) ask about controlled substance use; answering affirmatively may result in denial of firearm purchases
- **Existing firearms:** Individuals who already own firearms should consult with a legal attorney before beginning medical marijuana, as possession of both may create legal exposure

This is a significant consideration that should be discussed with Nicola Allen during your consultation if gun ownership or use is relevant to your situation.

### Other Potential Side Effects & Risks

- **Dizziness or dry mouth** from smoking or vaping
- **Impaired coordination or reaction time** — do not drive or operate machinery while impaired
- **Anxiety or paranoia** (particularly with high-THC strains)
- **Dependency potential,** especially with frequent or high-dose use
- **Cannabis Hyperemesis Syndrome** (excessive vomiting) in chronic heavy users
- **Mental health considerations:** History of psychosis or schizophrenia may increase risk with THC-heavy products
